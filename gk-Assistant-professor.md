### Topic: Ancient Civilizations & Archaeological Discoveries

**Q1.** The Archaeological Survey of India (ASI) excavation at Bahaj village in Deeg district, which started on January 10, 2024, reached a record depth. What is the depth achieved, making it the deepest archaeological dig in Rajasthan's history?

a) 15 meters
b) 20 meters
c) 23 meters
d) 28 meters

**Answer:** c) 23 meters

**Explanation:** The ASI excavation at Bahaj village reached a depth of 23 meters, uncovering a paleo-channel (ancient riverbed) potentially linked to the mythical Saraswati River. This excavation continued until May/June 2025 and is the deepest archaeological dig in Rajasthan's history.

**Why This Question is Important:**
1. **Record-breaking discovery:** This is a landmark archaeological achievement that showcases Rajasthan's rich ancient history, making it highly relevant for competitive exams.
2. **Recent development:** The excavation started in January 2024 and continued through 2025, representing cutting-edge archaeological research that examiners are likely to test.
3. **Multi-disciplinary significance:** The discovery connects archaeology, geography (paleo-channel), and ancient texts (Saraswati River), making it a comprehensive question topic.
